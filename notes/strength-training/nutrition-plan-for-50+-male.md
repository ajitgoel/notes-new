### 1. Estimated Calorie & Protein Needs
To achieve **moderate fat loss** (about 0.5 to 1 lb per week) while preserving muscle at age 50+, we need a slight caloric deficit but a high protein intake to combat age-related anabolic resistance (the body becomes less efficient at using protein to build muscle as we age).

*   **Daily Calories:** **~1,800 – 1,900 kcal/day** 
    *(Assuming a maintenance level of around 2,200–2,300 kcal based on your height, weight, and exercising once a week).*
*   **Daily Protein:** **140g – 160g per day** 
    *(Aiming for ~0.8 to 0.9 grams per pound of body weight. This is non-negotiable for preserving your strength while losing fat).*
*   **Fats & Carbs:** Keep fats moderate (~50-60g, mostly from egg yolks, ghee/oil used in cooking, nuts) and fill the rest with complex carbohydrates (~180-200g) to fuel your training.

### 2. Daily Meal Structure (Indian-Style + Eggs)
Right now, your 4 eggs provide about 24g of protein. Traditional Indian home-cooked meals (dal, roti, rice, sabzi) are highly nutritious but generally very carb-heavy and low in bioavailable protein. To hit 140g+ of protein without overshooting your calories, you need to make deliberate protein upgrades.

Here is a simple daily structure:

*   **Breakfast:** 
    *   2 whole eggs + 2 egg whites (scrambled or made into an Indian-style bhurji).
    *   2 Besan chillas (chickpea flour pancakes) OR 1 bowl of oats/dalia.
    *   *Protein: ~25g*
*   **Lunch:** 
    *   1 whole egg (boiled).
    *   1 large bowl of thick Dal or Chole (chickpeas).
    *   1 cup of Sabzi (mixed vegetables for fiber).
    *   1-2 Rotis.
    *   1/2 cup of Dahi (curd) or Greek yogurt.
    *   *Protein: ~25g*
*   **Afternoon Snack (Crucial Protein Boost):** 
    *   **1 scoop of Whey Protein Isolate** mixed in water or milk. *(Highly recommended: At 50+, whey is the most efficient, low-calorie way to get 25g of high-quality, fast-absorbing protein).*
    *   A small handful of roasted chana or almonds.
    *   *Protein: ~30g*
*   **Dinner:** 
    *   1 whole egg.
    *   100g Paneer (grilled/tikka) OR 50g Soya Chunks (Nutrela) cooked with veggies OR 150g Chicken breast (if you eat meat).
    *   Large portion of salad (cucumbers, tomatoes, onions).
    *   1 Roti or 1/2 cup of rice.
    *   *Protein: ~30-40g*

### 3. Pre- and Post-Workout Nutrition
Your extreme fatigue is likely a mix of central nervous system (CNS) drain and glycogen (stored carbohydrate) depletion. 

*   **Pre-Workout (1.5 to 2 hours before):** You need easily digestible carbs. Have a banana, a small bowl of oats, or 1-2 slices of toast with a little peanut butter, plus your morning eggs. 
*   **Intra-Workout (During):** Sip on water mixed with an electrolyte powder (or a pinch of salt, a squeeze of lemon, and a teaspoon of honey). High-rep leg presses will make you sweat out sodium, leading to a crash later.
*   **Post-Workout (Within 45 mins):** Your body is crying out for nutrients to repair the massive micro-tears from your workout. Have your **Whey Protein shake** immediately, along with a piece of fruit (like an apple or banana). Follow this up an hour later with a balanced meal (like your lunch).

### 4. Red Flags & Coaching Suggestions

*   **Red Flag #1: Workout Volume & Frequency.** You have labeled this "Day 2 - lower body", but you are doing Arnold presses, lat pulldowns, and two types of chest presses. This is a heavy **Full-Body** workout. Cramming 15-25 rep sets of heavy leg presses alongside heavy upper body work into a single day is a massive shock to your nervous system. 
    *   *The Fix:* **Split this into two shorter workouts per week.** For example, do Upper Body on Wednesday (Chest press, Lat pulldown, Arnold press) and Lower Body on Saturday (Leg press, Calf raise, plus core). You will stimulate muscle growth twice a week, and your post-workout fatigue will drop dramatically.
*   **Red Flag #2: Age-Related Recovery.** At 50+, tendons and ligaments take longer to recover than muscles. Doing 45 reps of calf raises or 25 reps of heavy leg presses creates immense systemic fatigue. Consider lowering the rep ranges to 8-12 reps with slightly heavier, controlled weight to protect your joints and reduce cardiovascular exhaustion.
*   **Red Flag #3: Sleep.** If you are not getting 7-8 hours of quality sleep, especially the night after this workout, your cortisol (stress hormone) will remain elevated, blocking fat loss and muscle repair. 

### 5. Your 4–6 Week Actionable Plan

**Week 1-2: Nutrition Baseline**
1.  **Track your protein:** Focus entirely on hitting 140g of protein daily. Buy a high-quality whey protein isolate (it is safe, effective, and vegetarian) to close the gap between your eggs/dal and your daily goal.
2.  **Hydrate:** Drink 3 liters of water daily. Add electrolytes on your workout day.

**Week 3-4: Training Adjustment**
1.  **Split the routine:** Try splitting your massive 1-day workout into two 40-minute sessions. You will lift the same total weekly volume, but your energy levels will skyrocket because you aren't draining your battery to 0% in one go.
2.  **Pre/Post Workout Habit:** Lock in the habit of eating a carb-rich snack before lifting and a protein shake immediately after. 

**Week 5-6: Monitor & Adjust**
1.  Weigh yourself 2-3 times a week in the morning and take the weekly average. If you are losing 0.5 to 1 lb a week, your calories are perfect. 
2.  If your weight isn't dropping, slightly reduce the carbs in your dinner (e.g., skip the rice/roti at night and double the veggies) while keeping protein high.

By spreading out your training stress and drastically increasing your daily protein intake, you will preserve your hard-earned strength, drop body fat, and stop feeling "run over by a truck" after you lift.
