**PROGRESSION WEEKS 1-4:**

- **Week 1:** Establish baseline loads at RPE 7-8 (2-3 reps left in tank)
- **Week 2:** Add 5 lb to compounds, add 1-2 reps to accessories
- **Week 3:** Add 5 lb to compounds OR add 1 set to lagging movements
- **Week 4:** Deload—reduce weight by 20% or reduce sets to 2 per exercise, maintain technique

---

#### **WEEK 5-8: BUILDING PHASE**

**Changes from Foundation:**

- Add 1 set to main compounds (squats, deadlifts, bench, overhead press = 5 sets)
- Increase intensity: RPE 8-9 on compounds
- Add 5-10 lb to most movements from Week 4 baseline
- Reduce rest on accessories to 45-60 sec (metabolic stress)

**New Exercise Additions (rotate 1-2 per workout):**

- Upper: Add dips or close-grip bench (triceps), barbell curls
- Lower: Add hack squat or front squat variation, glute-ham raises if available

**Weekly Progression:**

- Increase load 2.5-5 lb weekly on compounds
- Increase reps before adding load on accessories (e.g., 3×10 → 3×12 → 3×14, then add weight)

---

#### **WEEK 9-12: PEAKING PHASE**

**Changes:**

- Week 9: Deload (reduce volume by 40%, keep intensity moderate)
- Weeks 10-12: Push final progressive overload
    - Compounds: 5-6 × 6-8 reps at RPE 9
    - Increase load every week (even 2.5 lb matters)
    - Accessories: 3-4 × 10-12, focus on perfect form and contraction

**Goal Targets by Week 12:**

- DB Bench: 80-85 lb × 8
- Lat Pulldown: Bodyweight (178 lb) × 8 or assisted pull-ups
- Squat: 185 lb × 8
- RDL: 185 lb × 8
- Overhead Press: 60 lb DBs × 8

## PART 3: NUTRITION PLAN

### DAILY TARGETS (Recomposition Focus)

**Calories:** 2,200-2,300 per day

- Moderate deficit (~300-400 below maintenance) to prioritize fat loss while supporting muscle growth in a relatively untrained state

**Macros:**

- **Protein:** 180-200g/day (1.0-1.12g/lb bodyweight)
    - **Minimum per meal:** 35-45g × 4-5 meals
    - **Rationale:** Higher protein supports muscle protein synthesis, satiety, and thermic effect
- **Carbs:** 180-200g/day
    - Time 50-60g around training (pre/post)
- **Fats:** 60-70g/day
    - Essential for hormone production, vitamin absorption

**Sample Daily Breakdown:**

- Pre-workout: 40g carbs, 20g protein
- Post-workout: 45g protein, 50g carbs
- Remaining meals: distribute remaining protein and moderate carbs/fats

## PART 4: SUPPLEMENTS (Conservative, Evidence-Based)

### TIER 1 – HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

**1. Creatine Monohydrate**

- **Dose:** 5g/day, every day (timing doesn't matter)
- **Rationale:** Increases strength, power output, muscle cell hydration; helps with progressive overload
- **Vegetarian note:** Vegetarians often have lower baseline creatine stores, so response can be excellent
- **Form:** Unflavored powder in water or shake

**2. Vitamin B12**

- **Dose:** 1,000-2,500 mcg/day or 2,500 mcg 2-3×/week
- **Rationale:** Critical for vegetarians (no dietary source except fortified foods); supports energy and red blood cell production
- **Form:** Methylcobalamin or cyanocobalamin sublingual or capsule

**3. Vitamin D3**

- **Dose:** 2,000-4,000 IU/day (test levels ideally; aim for 40-60 ng/mL)
- **Rationale:** Supports testosterone, bone health, immune function, mood
- **Form:** Vegan D3 (lichen-derived) with a fat-containing meal

---

### TIER 2 – BENEFICIAL IF DIET GAPS EXIST

**4. Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)**

- **Dose:** 1-2g combined EPA/DHA daily
- **Rationale:** Reduces inflammation, supports joint health, cardiovascular health
- **Vegetarian source:** Algae-based omega-3 (e.g., Nordic Naturals Algae Omega, Ovega-3)

**5. Zinc**

- **Dose:** 15-30 mg/day (if not getting enough from diet)
- **Rationale:** Supports testosterone, immune function, protein synthesis
- **Food sources:** Pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds, chickpeas, lentils
- **Note:** Take with food to avoid nausea

**6. Iron** (only if blood test shows deficiency)

- **Dose:** 18-25 mg/day for males (get tested first)
- **Rationale:** Vegetarians can have lower iron; critical for oxygen transport and energy
- **Form:** Ferrous bisglycinate (gentle on stomach)
- **Pair with vitamin C:** Enhances absorption

---

### TIER 3 – OPTIONAL PERFORMANCE ENHANCERS

**7. Caffeine**

- **Dose:** 200-400 mg pre-workout (if tolerated)
- **Rationale:** Increases strength, endurance, focus
- **Timing:** 30-45 min before training
- **Note:** Cycle off 1-2 days/week to prevent tolerance

**8. Beta-Alanine**

- **Dose:** 3-5g/day (daily, timing irrelevant)
- **Rationale:** Buffers lactic acid, improves muscular endurance in 8-15 rep range
- **Side effect:** Harmless tingling (paresthesia)
