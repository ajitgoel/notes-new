### 2) The Training Program

**Philosophy:** Progressive Overload. You must do more (weight or reps) than the week before.
*   **Rest between sets:** 90–120 seconds for big lifts; 60 seconds for isolation.
*   **Tempo:** 3-0-1-0 (3 seconds lowering the weight, 0 pause, 1 second up, 0 pause). **Control the negative.**

#### Option A: 4-Day Split (Best for maximum visual change)
*Recommended Schedule: Mon (Upper), Tue (Lower), Thu (Upper), Fri (Lower)*

**Upper A (Focus: Chest/Back Width)**
1.  **Dumbbell Chest Press:** 3 sets × 8–10 reps (Go heavy. If you hit 10, increase weight).
2.  **Lat Pulldowns (Wide Grip):** 3 sets × 10–12 reps.
3.  **Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press:** 3 sets × 8–10 reps.
4.  **Cable Face Pulls (Posture):** 3 sets × 15 reps (Focus on squeezing shoulder blades).
5.  **Tricep Rope Pushdowns:** 3 sets × 12–15 reps.
6.  *Core:* Plank: 3 sets × max time (aim for 60s).

**Lower A (Focus: Quads/Calves)**
1.  **Goblet Squat or Leg Press:** 3 sets × 8–10 reps (Go deep).
2.  **Walking Lunges:** 3 sets × 10 steps per leg.
3.  **Leg Extension:** 3 sets × 12–15 reps.
4.  **Standing Calf Raises:** 4 sets × 10–12 reps (Heavy, slow eccentric).
5.  *Cardio:* 15 mins Incline Walk (10–12% incline, 3.0 speed).

**Upper B (Focus: Back Thickness/Arms)**
1.  **Seated Cable Row (or Chest Supported Machine Row):** 3 sets × 10–12 reps.
2.  **Incline Dumbbell Press:** 3 sets × 8–10 reps.
3.  **Dumbbell Lateral Raise:** 3 sets × 15 reps.
4.  **Dumbbell Bicep Curls:** 3 sets × 10–12 reps.
5.  **Pec Fly (Machine):** 2 sets × 15 reps.
6.  *Core:* Hanging Knee Raise (or Captain's Chair): 3 sets × 10–12 reps.

**Lower B (Focus: Hams/Glutes)**

#### Progression & Translating Your Current Loads
You listed "70 lb x 15" for DB Bench. If that is 70 lbs *per hand*, you are very strong but likely using momentum. If it is 70 lbs *total* (35 per hand), that is more consistent with your other numbers.
*   **Rule:** If you can do 15 reps easily, the weight is too light.
*   **New Target:** Pick a weight where you *cannot* do the 11th rep with perfect form.
*   **Week 1:** Find your baselines.
*   **Weeks 2–4:** Add reps (e.g., if you did 50lbs for 8 reps, try to get 9 or 10 reps).
*   **Weeks 5–8:** Add weight (once you hit the top of the rep range, add 5lbs and go back to the bottom of the rep range).
