## 9. Risk & Compliance

### Brand Safety Guidelines

**Do:**

- Showcase authentic food prep, real customers (with consent), genuine chef stories
- Use licensed music (TikTok/IG library, Epidemic Sound if budget allows Month 2+)
- Disclose partnerships/gifted meals with #ad, #partner, #gifted per FTC
- Respond professionally to negative reviews (never argue, always offer resolution)

**Don't:**

- Use copyrighted music outside platform libraries (risk takedowns)
- Make health claims ("boosts immunity," "cures…") without FDA compliance
- Compare competitors directly by name ("Better than [Restaurant X]")
- Film customers without visible consent (verbal on-camera or signed waiver)

---

### Music & Licensing (Reels/TikTok)

**Safe Options:**

1. **TikTok/Instagram Music Library:** Use trending audio directly in-app (pre-licensed)
2. **Epidemic Sound ($15/mo):** Royalty-free music for commercial use (Month 2+ if budget)
3. **Original Audio:** Chef talking, ASMR food sounds (no music) = zero risk

**Risky:** Adding Spotify tracks or YouTube rips → platforms will mute/remove.

---

### Review Platform Policies

**Google:**

- Prohibits incentivizing reviews (no "Free appetizer for 5-star review")
- Allows asking neutrally ("Share your experience")
- Requires responding to reviews (builds trust + SEO)

**Yelp:**

- Similar to Google; very strict on incentivization
- Discouraged: Mass email review requests (Yelp may filter as "solicited")
- Encouraged: Organic post-visit asks, responding to reviews

**Strategy:** Always ask for feedback first, let customer choose platform (never direct "Go to Google only").

---

### Data Privacy (Toast Customer Data)

- **Email/SMS Opt-In:** Ensure Toast signup has clear consent ("Get offers + updates")
- **Unsubscribe Link:** Required in every marketing email (Toast auto-includes)
- **No Data Selling:** Never share customer lists with third parties

---

### Ad Compliance (Meta/TikTok)

**Prohibited:**

- Before/after body images (if promoting diet benefits)
- Alcohol ads targeting <21 (if showcasing wine pairings)
- Misleading claims ("Healthiest restaurant," "Doctor-recommended")

**Required:**

- Accurate pricing (don't bait with "$10 meals" if not available)
- Clear CTAs ("Order," "Reserve," "Learn More"—not vague "Click Here")
