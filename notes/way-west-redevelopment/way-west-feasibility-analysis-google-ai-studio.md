## 1. Site & Zoning Context

### **Zoning Overview**
*   **Zone:** **NC3P-75 (M)** (Neighborhood Commercial 3, Pedestrian-Designated, 75’ Height Limit, Mandatory Housing Affordability).
*   **Lot Size:** ~19,933 SF (0.46 acres).
*   **Overlay:** Aurora-Licton Springs Residential Urban Village.

### **Development Capacity**
*   **Max Height:** 75 feet (allows for 7 stories).
    *   *Optimal Structure:* 5 stories of Type V-A (wood) over 2 stories of Type I-A (concrete podium).
*   **FAR (Floor Area Ratio):** Approx. 5.5 FAR for mixed-use.
    *   *Buildable Gross SF:* ~105,000 – 110,000 Gross SF.
*   **Ground Floor Requirements ("P" Suffix):**
    *   The "P" (Pedestrian) designation is critical. You **cannot** put residential units on the street-facing ground level along Aurora.
    *   You must provide typical "commercial" uses (retail, office, services) for at least 80% of the Aurora frontage.
*   **Parking:**
    *   **Requirement:** **None.** (Frequent Transit Service Area).
    *   **Recommendation:** Market demand on Aurora dictates providing parking (see Section 6).
*   **MHA (Mandatory Housing Affordability):**
    *   **Tier:** "M" (Medium).
    *   **Obligation:** You must either include ~9% affordable units (performance) or pay a fee-in-lieu.
    *   **Strategy:** **Pay the Fee.** MHA fees in this zone (Area C) are approx. **$35.00/sq ft** of chargeable floor area. This allows you to build 100% market-rate units and simplifies disposition/sale later.

### **Estimated Project Scale**
*   **Total Unit Count:** **130 – 145 Units**
*   **Net Rentable SF (Residential):** ~78,000 SF
*   **Commercial SF:** ~3,500 SF (Minimizing to meet code)

## 2. Submarket & Demand Analysis

### **The "Aurora-Licton" Value Proposition**
This submarket acts as a **pricing relief valve** for Green Lake and Greenwood. Renters here are price-sensitive but demand connectivity. They are willing to tolerate the "grit" of Aurora Ave for a $300-$500/month discount compared to buildings three blocks west.

### **Target Renter Profile**
1.  **The "Start-Up" Renter:** Young professionals (22-28) working in SLU or Downtown who use the E-Line Bus (RapidRide) and can't afford a $2,400 studio in Capitol Hill.
2.  **Medical/Northgate Workers:** Staff from UW Medical Center Northwest (nearby) and Northgate retail/service workers.
3.  **North Seattle College Students:** The campus is walking distance (via the pedestrian bridge).

### **Market Metrics (Q1 2026 Estimates)**
*   **Vacancy:** ~5.5% (Stabilized).
*   **Rent Growth:** Projected 2.5% - 3.0% YoY.
*   **Absorption:** High for studios/open 1-beds; slower for large 2-beds ($2,600+ price point hits a ceiling here).

## 4. Optimal Unit Mix & Sizing

**Strategy:** Maximize density while avoiding "micro-unit" fatigue. Focus on **"Urban 1-Bedrooms"** (Open 1s) which lease fastest to single professionals.

| Unit Type | % of Total | Count | Avg Size (SF) | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Studio (Alcove)** | 25% | ~34 | 380 - 410 | Entry-level price point. Must have defined sleeping nook (no shoeboxes). |
| **Open 1-Bed** | 45% | ~60 | 480 - 520 | **The Sweet Spot.** Sliding glass doors for bedroom. Perceived as a 1BR, priced between Studio/1BR. |
| **Traditional 1-Bed** | 20% | ~27 | 600 - 630 | For couples. Corner units with better light. |
| **2-Bed / 1-Bath** | 10% | ~14 | 750 - 800 | Roommates/students. Keep to 1 bath to save plumbing costs and keep rent gross $ lower. |

*Avoid 3-bedroom units; the rent required to justify the SF exceeds the mortgage payment of a starter condo in this area.*

## 6. Parking, Mobility & Transit Strategy

**The Aurora Paradox:** Zoning requires 0 parking, but tenants on Aurora often own cars because the environment is hostile to walking at night, despite good bus service.

*   **Recommendation:** **0.4 to 0.5 Ratio (approx. 55-65 stalls).**
*   **Design:** Two levels of parking:
    1.  At-grade (behind the commercial retail).
    2.  One level of below-grade or semi-depressed parking.
    *   *Avoid deep excavation if possible to save cost.*
*   **Pricing:** **Unbundled.** Charge $150-$175/month per stall. If you bundle it, you inflate the headline rent and lose prospects.
*   **Transit Pitch:** Market the **RapidRide E Line** aggressively ("12 mins to SLU"). Install a "Transit Screen" in the lobby showing real-time bus arrivals.

## 7. Ground-Floor Program & Amenities

### **Commercial Space (The "P" Zone Requirement)**
*   **Risk:** Retail vacancy on Aurora is high.
*   **Strategy:** Design ~3,000 SF of flexible commercial depth.
*   **Tenant Target:** "Destination Service" that does not rely on foot traffic.
    *   *Ideal:* Vet clinic, physical therapy, or a local non-profit office.
    *   *Avoid:* High-end boutique retail or restaurants (venting costs are high and failure rate is high here).

### **Residential Amenities (ROI Focused)**
1.  **Package Security (Critical):** Amazon Hub/Luxer One lockers in a secure room. Package theft is the #1 complaint in this area.
2.  **Gym:** 600 SF is sufficient. Focus on heavy weights and treadmills.
3.  **Roof Deck:** The **secret weapon** of this site. You have elevation. A roof deck here will have stunning views of the Cascades and potential Green Lake glimpses. Include fire pits and BBQs.
4.  **Co-Working Lobby:** Make the lobby a "Third Place" with booths and high-speed Wi-Fi.

---

## 8. Design & Positioning Strategy (Aurora-Specific)

### **Mitigating "The Highway"**
1.  **Acoustics are Paramount:**
    *   **STC 35+ Windows:** Triple-pane or laminated glass on the Aurora-facing façade is mandatory.
    *   **Trickle Vents:** Ensure fresh air intake doesn't require opening windows to street noise.
2.  **Building Orientation:**
    *   **"C" or "U" Shape:** Orient the courtyard away from Aurora (facing East/Nesbit Ave).
    *   **The "Quiet Side" Premium:** Charge a $50-$75 premium for East-facing units (quiet + morning sun).
3.  **Security Perception:**
    *   Bright lighting on all facades (no dark corners).
    *   Fob-access gates at all pedestrian entry points.
    *   Camera coverage visible in the lobby to reassure residents.

## 9. Phasing, Timing & Delivery

*   **Entitlements (MUP):** 12-16 months (Seattle is slow).
*   **Permitting:** 6-8 months concurrent.
*   **Construction:** 20-22 months (Type V over I).
*   **Target Start:** **Q2 2027**.
*   **Target Delivery:** **Q1 2029**.
    *   *Why:* There is a current glut of supply delivering in 2025/26. By 2029, the "supply cliff" will hit (due to current high interest rates stalling starts), allowing you to deliver into a high-rent-growth market.

## 10. Final Recommendation: "The Licton Link"

If this were my project, I would execute the following:

**The Product:**
A 135-unit, 7-story building branded as **"The Licton Link"** or similar. It bridges the gap between the connectivity of Northgate/Light Rail and the recreation of Green Lake.

**The "Winning" Formula:**
1.  **Pay the MHA Fee:** Do not build affordable units on-site; maximize revenue per SF with market-rate efficiency units.
2.  **Lean into Efficiency:** The **Open 1-Bedroom (500 SF)** is your bread and butter. It appeals to the hybrid worker who needs a separation of space but can't afford a true 1-bed.
3.  **Fortress Design:** Spend the extra budget on **soundproofing and security**. Marketing a "Sound-Proof Sanctuary" on Aurora is a unique selling proposition that competitors ignore.
4.  **East-Facing Courtyard:** Focus the resident experience toward the quiet, residential neighborhood of Licton Springs to the east, turning the building's back to the highway.

**Financial Snapshot:**
*   **Est. Hard/Soft Cost:** ~$375k - $400k per door.
*   **Yield on Cost:** Aiming for **5.75% - 6.0%** untrended (solid for Seattle proper).

This project works because it serves the "Missing Middle" workforce renter who has been priced out of the Class-A towers but refuses to live in 1970s garden-style walk-ups.
