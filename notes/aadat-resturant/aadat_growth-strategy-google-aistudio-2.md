**The Flaw:** The plan assumes you can sell *Indian Buffet packages* for Quinceañeras.
**The Reality:** While Latinos love Indian food, they rarely want Butter Chicken and Naan as the primary meal for a traditional Quinceañera (which usually requires Birria, Carnitas, Rice, and Beans).
**The Fix:** You must pivot the Banquet Hall strategy for Latinos to **"Venue Rental + Outside Catering Allowed"** OR develop a **"Continental/Italian Menu"** option.
*   **Action:** Market the hall to Latinos as "The most elegant hall that allows *your* food" (charging a higher rental fee to compensate for lost food sales) or partner with a local Mexican caterer who uses your kitchen.

### 2. The "Halal vs. Hotel Bar" Friction (Cultural Nuance)
**The Flaw:** The plan suggests simply getting Halal certification will bring the Somali community back.
**The Reality:** You are inside a **Four Points by Sheraton**. If there is a visible bar or alcohol served in the main dining room, strictly religious Somali families (especially elders) may still avoid it *even with* Halal meat certification.
**The Fix:** You need **Spatial Segmentation**.
*   **Action:** If possible, designate the Banquet Hall (when not in use) or a specific section of the restaurant as an **"Alcohol-Free Family Zone"** on Friday nights/weekends. Market this specific zone to the mosques.

### 3. Operational Overload (Staffing Reality)
**The Flaw:** The plan suggests launching Wedding Packages, Corporate Catering, and Quinceañeras simultaneously in Month 1.
**The Reality:** Doing all three requires a dedicated Sales Manager. If the owner/GM tries to do this while running the floor, they will burn out, and inquiries will slip through the cracks.
**The Fix:** **Sequence the launch.** Focus on **Buffet Volume (Somali recovery)** first to build cash flow, *then* hire a commission-based Event Sales rep to handle the Banquet Hall.
