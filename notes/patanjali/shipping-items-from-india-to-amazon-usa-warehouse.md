Yes, this is feasible via **Amazon Global Logistics** or a third‑party freight forwarder. The critical constraints are US import compliance, Amazon’s FBA prep/label rules, and correct shipment creation in Seller Central so the FC will accept the freight.

**What you must have in place** 

- **Importer of Record (IOR):** A US EIN and a customs bond (single‑entry or continuous). Amazon will not be IOR or pay duties; use **DDP Incoterms** so all charges are prepaid.
- **Customs broker/freight forwarder:** Handle air/ocean, clearance, duties, and delivery appointments to FCs.
- **FDA/US compliance for your categories:**

- **Supplements/foods (Nutrela, chyawanprash, honey, ghee):** FDA facility registration, a US agent, **Prior Notice** for each shipment, compliant English labels with Supplement Facts/Nutrition Facts, **lot/batch** and **expiry**, **country of origin**, and no disease claims.
- **Dairy (ghee):** FDA‑regulated; ensure correct **HS code**, admissibility, and residue/composition compliance.
- **Cosmetics/personal care:** INCI ingredient list, net contents, manufacturer, country of origin; ensure claims are non‑drug.

- **Amazon Brand Registry** (recommended for Vine, content protection) and category ungating if applicable.

**FBA prep and labeling essentials** 

- **FNSKU** barcode on each sellable unit; **scannable UPC/GTIN** if used must map to the listing.
- **Expiry** printed in MM‑YYYY (or DD‑MMM‑YYYY) and visible; Amazon often requires ≥90–180 days remaining.
- **Unit prep:** poly‑bag with **suffocation warning** if needed; seal consumables; bubble wrap for fragile; no leaking; meltable goods follow temperature windows.
- **Carton/pallet rules:** Max 50 lb per carton (unless single unit over 50 lb), correct **carton labels** from the shipment plan, ISPM‑15 heat‑treated pallets for wood.

**Shipping options from India** 

- **Amazon Global Logistics (AGL):** Door‑to‑door air/ocean integrated with Seller Central; creates shipment IDs and FC appointments.
- **Third‑party forwarder:** Use your shipment plan in Seller Central, then have your forwarder schedule FC delivery (non‑partnered carrier) after customs clearance.

**The process, end‑to‑end** 

1. **Create FBA listings** and **shipment plan** in Seller Central to the assigned US fulfillment centers; download unit and carton labels.
2. **Prep and label** inventory to Amazon standards; confirm expiry buffers for consumables.
3. **Book freight** (AGL or forwarder), choose **DDP**; your broker files **FDA Prior Notice** and handles customs.
4. **Customs documents:** Commercial invoice, packing list, HS codes, FDA PN confirmations, facility registration/US agent details, and any certificates requested.
5. **Deliver to FCs:** Carrier obtains delivery appointments; Amazon receives and stows inventory.

**Practical cautions** 

- **Amazon won’t accept collect freight or act as consignee/IOR.** Duties/taxes must be prepaid.
- **Label compliance is non‑negotiable** for supplements/foods; missing Supplement Facts, dosage, or warnings can lead to refusal.
- **Shelf life matters:** Amazon may reject short‑dated goods; plan production dating accordingly.
- **Hazmat review:** Some oils or glass packaging can trigger additional prep or hazmat checks.

In short, direct India‑to‑US FBA is straightforward if you handle **IOR, FDA Prior Notice, DDP freight**, and adhere to **Amazon FBA prep/**
