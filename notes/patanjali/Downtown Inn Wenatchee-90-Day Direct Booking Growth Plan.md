## 9. RISK MITIGATION & COMPLIANCE

### Hotel Advertising Policy Compliance

**Google Ads Hotel Policies:**

- ✓ Accurate pricing displayed (no bait-and-switch)
- ✓ Clear cancellation policy linked from ads
- ✓ No false scarcity claims (only use real inventory data)
- ✓ Privacy policy and terms linked in footer

**Meta Advertising Policies:**

- ✓ No discriminatory targeting (ensure age/gender targeting is inclusive)
- ✓ Landing page matches ad promise (direct to room booking, not generic homepage)
- ✓ Contact information visible (phone, address, email)

### Rate Parity & OTA Compliance

**Current Risk:** OTAs may penalize you for advertising lower public rates; most contracts require rate parity.

**Mitigation Strategy:**

1. **Match Public Rates:** Keep downtowninnwa.com public rates equal to OTA rates
2. **Differentiate with Value:** Offer free parking, late checkout, or other perks not available on OTAs (legal under most contracts)
3. **Closed User Group Discounts:** Offer "member rates" via email signup (10-15% off) - not publicly visible, typically allowed
4. **Package Rates:** Bundle room + parking + breakfast equivalent for higher perceived value without violating rate parity
5. **Last-Room Availability:** Close inventory on OTAs during high-demand periods; sell last rooms direct at premium

**Audit Quarterly:**

- Check your rates on Booking.com, Expedia, Google Hotels vs. your site
- Document any discrepancies and adjust

### Reputation & Review Management

**Current State:** Strong reviews are your competitive advantage. Protect them.

**Safeguards:**

1. **Proactive Review Requests:** Send post-stay email (via Cloudbeds or manual) asking satisfied guests to review on Google
2. **Rapid Response Protocol:** Respond to all reviews within 24-48 hours; thank positive reviews, address negative reviews constructively
3. **Service Recovery Budget:** Allocate $200/month for service recovery (refunds, comp nights) to prevent negative reviews from escalating
4. **Staff Training:** Weekly 15-minute huddle on guest service standards; review recent feedback

**Target:**

- Maintain 4.5+ star average on Google
- Increase review velocity to 8-12/month (currently ~3-5 assumed)
- <5% negative reviews; respond to 100% of negative reviews

### Contingency Plans

**Scenario 1: Convention Center Event Cancellations**

- **Risk:** Major events postponed or canceled, reducing corporate demand
- **Mitigation:** Diversify beyond Convention Center; invest in leisure/long-term stay marketing; build email list for direct communication
- **Action:** If event demand drops >30%, reallocate $100-150/month from convention targeting to trail/leisure targeting

**Scenario 2: Google Ads Performance Below Target (ROAS <3x in Month 1)**

- **Risk:** High CPCs, low conversion rate, or poor keyword targeting
- **Mitigation:** Pause underperforming campaigns; shift budget to retargeting and GMB optimization (lower cost, higher intent)
- **Action:** Conduct keyword audit; add 20-30 negative keywords; test lower-funnel terms ("book hotel wenatchee" vs. "hotels wenatchee")

**Scenario 3: Website Technical Issues (Booking Engine Downtime)**

- **Risk:** Cloudbeds outage or payment processing errors lose bookings
- **Mitigation:** Add phone number prominently on all pages; train staff to take manual bookings; pause ads during known outages
- **Action:** Test booking flow weekly; maintain backup phone booking process; consider secondary payment processor

**Scenario 4: Competitive Pressure (New Hotel Opens or Major Competitor Launches Promotion)**

- **Risk:** Lost market share, downward rate pressure
- **Mitigation:** Double down on unique differentiators (Convention Center proximity, trail access, local charm); emphasize reviews and loyalty
- **Action:** Monitor competitor rates weekly; adjust promotions tactically; increase review generation efforts
