### **6. Compliance Checklist (Crucial for Indian Imports)**

*   **FDA Disclaimer:** Every supplement label *must* have the box: *"These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."*
*   **Supplement Facts Panel:** Must follow US format (not Indian format). Must list "Amount Per Serving" and "% Daily Value".
*   **Heavy Metals:** Indian herbal imports are under high scrutiny for lead/mercury. **Requirement:** Have a COA (Certificate of Analysis) from a **US-based lab** ready for Amazon. They *will* ask for it.
*   **Claims to AVOID:** "Anti-inflammatory" (Drug claim), "Pain Relief" (Drug claim), "Boosts Immunity" (Borderline—safer is "Supports Immune Health"), "Cures Insomnia" (Drug claim).
*   **Prop 65 (California):** If selling in CA, ensure you have a warning label if lead content is above the micro-limit, or ensure your product tests clean enough to be exempt.
