## Part 8: CRITICAL RISKS & MITIGATION

### **Risk 1: Container Gets Stuck at Port (FDA hold)**

**Probability:** 15-20% for first shipment  
**Impact:** $500-2,000 demurrage fees + delays

**Mitigation:**

1. Require lab certificates from Patanjali for:
    - Pesticide residue
    - Heavy metals (lead, arsenic)
    - Microbiological testing
2. Use customs broker experienced with Indian food imports
3. Have backup $5k cash for emergencies

---

### **Risk 2: Amazon Account Suspension**

**Probability:** 10-15% in Year 1 (common for new sellers)  
**Triggers:** Inauthentic complaints, IP violations, high defect rate

**Mitigation:**

1. Get written authorization from Patanjali to resell
2. Never sell Divya supplements (high suspension risk)
3. Poly-bag all liquids to prevent leakage
4. Respond to customer complaints within 24 hours
5. Keep 40% inventory in B2B channels (don't be 100% dependent on Amazon)

---

### **Risk 3: Patanjali Appoints Official US Distributor**

**Probability:** 30-40% within 2-3 years  
**Impact:** They could terminate your dealership

**Mitigation:**

1. Build customer relationships in B2B (they'll stay with you)
2. Diversify to other Indian brands (MTR, Haldiram's)
3. Create your own private label versions of best-sellers
4. Get 3-year minimum commitment in writing from Patanjali

---

### **Risk 4: Race to Bottom Pricing**

**Probability:** 80% on Amazon for commodity items  
**Impact:** Margins compress from 35% to 15%

**Mitigation:**

1. Set repricer floor at landed cost + $3 minimum
2. Focus on products with fewer Amazon sellers (Chyawanprash, specialty items)
3. Build brand value through content (recipes, Ayurveda education)
4. Bundle products (Ghee + Turmeric + Recipe book)
