*   *Why it matters:* Most modern scalable backends and Solution Architect JDs require deep knowledge of distributed messaging (Apache Kafka, AWS SQS/SNS, RabbitMQ, EventBridge). Your resume shows strong synchronous API (REST) and database skills, but lacks asynchronous event-driven experience.
*   *How to build it:* At Amazon, look for opportunities to integrate SQS, SNS, or EventBridge into your architecture. Alternatively, build a side project demonstrating an event-driven microservices pattern (e.g., an order processing system) utilizing Kafka or AWS SQS.

**2. Mentorship, Leadership, and Cross-Org Influence (High Priority)**
*   *Why it matters:* For Principal/Architect levels, companies expect you to act as a "force multiplier." Your resume says "Technical Leader," but lacks concrete examples of mentoring juniors, driving engineering standards across *multiple* teams, or resolving organizational technical debt.
*   *How to build it:* Start leading architectural review boards at Amazon. Mentor mid-level engineers and document it. Add bullets to your resume like, *"Mentored X engineers and established organizational standards for..."*

**3. Kubernetes (K8s) & Orchestration (Medium-High Priority)**
*   *Why it matters:* If you are targeting DevOps or Platform/Cloud Engineering roles outside of AWS ecosystem purists, Kubernetes is the industry standard. You have ECS and Docker, which is great, but K8s is heavily requested.
