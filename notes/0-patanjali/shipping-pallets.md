The cheapest option is usually LTL freight booked through a broker, not directly via FedEx/UPS.

Since you’re in Everett and just want to ship a pallet from A to B, think of two layers: how you move it (LTL freight) and who you use (local vs national vs online broker).

**1. What’s usually cheapest** 

For a standard pallet (under ~2,000 lb, US domestic):

- **LTL (Less‑Than‑Truckload) via an online marketplace/broker** like Shiply is often the lowest cost because carriers are filling unused space on routes they’re already running, so quotes can be **up to 75% cheaper than standard rates**.[​⁠](https://www.shiply.com/us/pallet-shipping?srsltid=AfmBOoqvrRIL-AJrNodlEtWnqX17fCCsAHvrTYR9fLYz8pvoXtScKzuG)
- Direct retail with FedEx Freight or UPS Freight tends to be **more expensive** unless you already have a discounted business account.
- If the shipment is local/short‑haul within WA, a regional carrier or a local moving/freight company can be cost‑competitive, especially if you care more about simplicity than rock‑bottom pricing.

    You’ll handle a bit more setup (measuring, labeling, being ready for the truck), but you’ll usually save the most money.

**3. How I’d approach it (practical steps)** 

1. **Prep the pallet**

- Use a standard 40” x 48” wood pallet.
- Stack boxes in columns, keep everything inside the pallet footprint (no overhang).
- Shrink‑wrap tightly and use straps if it’s heavy or tall. This is important for any LTL carrier.

3. **Get your shipment details ready**

- Pallet dimensions: length, width, and total height (including pallet).
- Exact weight (pallet + goods).
- Pickup and delivery addresses and whether each is **residential vs commercial**, and if there’s a dock or forklift. Liftgate and residential surcharges add a lot to cost, so if you can ship **business‑to‑business with docks**, you’ll save.

5. **Collect 2–3 quotes:**

- Use an online broker (e.g., Shiply) to get a baseline cheapest quote.
- Call or visit **PostalAnnex (Fred Meyer Center, 1523 132nd St SE)** and ask specifically for **LTL freight, cheapest option**, giving them your dimensions and weight.
- Optionally, get a quote from **The UPS Store on Evergreen Way** if you want to compare “we handle everything” options.

7. **Choose based on:**

- Total cost (including residential/liftgate fees).
- Transit time you’re okay with (economy vs faster).
- How much hand‑holding you want (local store vs online DIY).

**4. Simple rule of thumb** 

- **Max savings, okay doing some logistics yourself:** Online LTL broker (like Shiply) + you prepare the pallet and BOL.
- **Balance of cost and convenience, still relatively cheap:** PostalAnnex Everett as a freight forwarder to compare carriers.
- **Most convenient, not necessarily cheapest:** The UPS Store freight service on Evergreen Way.
