### **Execution Plan for a 2-Person Team**

#### **1. Brand Registry & Listing Optimization**
Since you have Brand Registry, you must use **A+ Content (EBC)**.
*   **Action:** Don't just list the product. Create a "Brand Story" module that appears on all your listings. Highlight Patanjali’s heritage, herbal roots, and "Made in India" authenticity.
*   **Bundle Listings:** Create unique ASINs for "Patanjali Morning Kit" (Toothpaste + Soap + Face Wash). This prevents competitors from hopping on your listing because they won't have the specific bundle packaging.

#### **2. Repricer Strategy**
Since you are using a repricer:
*   **Buy Box Wins:** For "Dant Kanti" and "Ghee," competition is fierce. Set your repricer to **"Target Buy Box"** but ensure your **Min Price** includes:
    *   Product Cost
    *   Shipping to Amazon
    *   Amazon Referral Fee (usually 8-15%)
    *   FBA Fulfillment Fee
    *   *Minimum 15% Profit Margin buffer.*
*   **Don't Race to the Bottom:** If the price drops below your Min Price, let the competitor sell out. They are likely losing money. Wait for them to go out of stock, then your price naturally takes over.

#### **3. Compliance & Risk Management (Crucial)**
*   **Divya Medicines (Vati/Kwath):** Be very careful. Amazon bots often flag these as "Unapproved Drugs."
    *   *Recommendation:* Start with the **Cosmetics and Foods** (Toothpaste, Soaps, Ghee) to build account health history before introducing the Supplements/Medicines.

#### **4. Amazon.in Proxy Data**
Use the Amazon India site to check the "Frequently Bought Together" section on Patanjali items.
*   *Example:* If customers on Amazon.in buy *Kesh Kanti Oil* with *Kesh Kanti Shampoo*, create that bundle for your FBA inventory.

Since you are a **2-person team** with the **official dealership** rights, you have a massive advantage: **Margin Control.**

You mentioned that "Amazon is a lot of work for a small amount of money." **This is only true if you sell single units.** If you sell one tube of toothpaste for $6.99, Amazon takes $4.00, and you make pennies.

To get maximum "Bang for the Buck" without hiring employees, you need to pivot your business model away from "Retailer" and toward **"Institutional Supplier"** and **"Bulk FBA."**

Here are 4 specific, high-leverage moves for a small team:

### 1. The "Temple & Gurdwara" Strategy (Institutional Sales)
This is the single highest ROI activity for a 2-person team on the West Coast.
*   **The Logic:** Temples consume massive amounts of **Ghee** (for lamps/diyas and prasad), **Incense (Agarbatti)**, and **Cleaning supplies** (Phenyl/Dishwash).
*   **The Play:** Do not sell them 500g jars. Sell them cases.
*   **Why it works:**
    *   **Zero Marketing Cost:** You just need to visit the Operations Manager of the top 10 Temples/Gurdwaras in CA/WA.
    *   **High Volume, One Drop:** You drop off 50 cases of Ghee and 20 cases of Incense in *one trip*.
    *   **Recurring Revenue:** They will need the same order every month. It’s automatic income.
*   **Target Products:** Patanjali Cow Ghee (Tins), Agarbatti, Dishwash Bar.

### 2. The "Jobber" Strategy (Outsource the Driving)
You cannot afford to drive a van to 100 small stores to restock 5 tubes of toothpaste. It’s a waste of your life.
*   **The Play:** Find the "Jobbers." These are the independent van drivers who already visit Indian grocery stores weekly to sell phone cards, snacks, or bread.
*   **The Deal:** Sell to the Jobbers at a wholesale rate (Cost + 10%). Let *them* sell to the small stores at Cost + 25%.
*   **Bang for Buck:** You become the "Wholesaler to the Wholesalers." You move pallets out of your garage/warehouse without ever sitting in traffic or chasing a $50 invoice from a small store.

### 3. The "Amazon Case Pack" Strategy
You said Amazon is small money. That is because you are likely selling consumer units (1 item). You need to sell **Reseller Packs**.
*   **The Logic:** There are hundreds of small Indian grocery stores in the Midwest and South (Texas, Florida, Ohio) that cannot reach you and don't have a local Patanjali distributor. They buy their stock *on Amazon*.
*   **The Play:** Create Amazon FBA listings specifically for them.
    *   *Instead of:* 1 Tube of Dant Kanti ($6.99)
    *   *Sell:* **"Retailer Pack: 12 Tubes of Dant Kanti" for $45.00.**
*   **Why it works:**
    *   **One FBA Fee:** Amazon charges the FBA pick/pack fee *once* for the box of 12, not 12 times. Your margin triples.
    *   **Zero Competition:** Most sellers are fighting for the single tube Buy Box. Few sell the bulk packs.
    *   **High AOV:** You make $45 sales instead of $7 sales.

### 4. The "American Yoga" Pivot (High Margin)
Patanjali is a "grocery brand" to Indians, but it is an "Exotic Ayurvedic Brand" to Americans. Americans will pay **3x the price** if the context is right.
*   **The Play:** Stop trying to sell **Shilajit** and **Ashwagandha** to Indian stores (who know the wholesale price is low).
*   **Target:** Independent Yoga Studios, Wellness Spas, and Acupuncture Clinics on the West Coast.
*   **The Pitch:** Offer them a "Counter Display Box" of Patanjali Shilajit or Balm.
*   **Bang for Buck:**
    *   Indian Store Price for Shilajit: $8.00 (They complain it's expensive).
    *   Yoga Studio Retail Price: $25.00 (They think it's a bargain compared to Whole Foods).
*   **Action:** Send a sample box to the top 20 Yoga studios in Los Angeles/San Francisco with a wholesale price list.
