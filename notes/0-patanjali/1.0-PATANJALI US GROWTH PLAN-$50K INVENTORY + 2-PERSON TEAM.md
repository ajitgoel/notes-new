## FDA/FTC COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS (US MARKET)

### PROHIBITED CLAIMS (NEVER USE)

| **Category** | **Prohibited Language** | **Why It's Risky** | **Penalty** |
|------------|----------------------|----------------|----------|
| **Disease Claims** | "Cures," "treats," "prevents," "heals" | Implies medical treatment (requires FDA approval) | Warning letter, product seizure |
| **Specific Diseases** | "Cancer," "diabetes," "COVID," "arthritis," "hypertension" | Unapproved drug claims | $500K+ fines, criminal charges |
| **Body Structure** | "Strengthens bones," "repairs tissue," "purifies blood" | Structure/function overreach | Warning letter |
| **Absolute Language** | "Guaranteed results," "clinically proven" (without trials) | FTC false advertising | Lawsuit, refunds |
| **Immune Claims** | "Boosts immunity," "prevents infection" | COVID-era FDA scrutiny (highly enforced) | Immediate takedown |

---

### ALLOWED CLAIMS (SAFE WITH DISCLAIMER)

✅ **Structure/Function Claims** (must add FDA disclaimer):
- "Supports [organ/system] health*"
- "Promotes [normal bodily function]*"
- "Helps maintain [physiological state]*"
- "Traditional use for [general wellness]*"

✅ **Nutrient Content Claims**:
- "Good source of Vitamin C" (if ≥10% DV)
- "High in antioxidants"
- "Contains 600mg ashwagandha per serving"

✅ **General Wellness**:
- "Supports overall wellness*"
- "Part of a healthy lifestyle*"
- "May help maintain [already normal levels]*"

## IF LISTING IS REJECTED OR FLAGGED

**Amazon Policy Violation Notice:**

1. **Do not argue** with Amazon Support (wastes time, may escalate)
2. **Acknowledge the issue**: "Thank you for the feedback. We will revise immediately."
3. **Rewrite with more conservative language** (use "safe copy" templates above)
4. **Resubmit within 24 hours** (delays cost sales)
5. **Appeal only if** you believe the flag is erroneous (rare)

**Common Rejection Reasons:**
- Missing FDA disclaimer (supplements)
- Disease claims in title or bullets
- Competitor complaints (fake or genuine)
- Image contains health claims (move text to description only)

---

## FINAL COMPLIANCE NOTES

**Conservative = Safe = Revenue**  
It's better to under-claim and keep listings live than to over-claim and risk suspension. Competitors may use risky language, but that doesn't mean you should—they may not have been caught yet.

**Amazon vs D2C:**  
You can be slightly more detailed on your own website (patanjaliayurvedus.com), but still avoid disease claims. Amazon has stricter enforcement because they're liable.

**When in Doubt, Add Disclaimer:**  
If a claim feels borderline, add "*" and link to the FDA disclaimer. This signals compliance intent.

**Quarterly Review:**  
Every 3 months, audit all listings for new FDA/FTC guidance or platform policy changes. Compliance is ongoing, not one-time.
